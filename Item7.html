Radio work

Soldered Radio + 3d printed case

45~ minutes

This is a radio I soldered from a radio kit. Right away, I noticed that my light wouldnâ€™t turn on when the radio was on, I attempted to remedy this by switching the LED, however, that did not resolve the problem. After attempting to troubleshoot the problem, I decided that it was not a large enough problem to ruin the project. The second issue I encountered happened after I got the radio working. The battery kept unseating itself, requiring me to resolder it. After a while, the radio stopped working altogether, however, im unsure what the exact problem was. If I could redo it, I would most likely pay closer attention to the antenna and the battery pack, as both seemed to be possible failure points.

For the second part of this project, I created a 3d printed design enclosure for the radio. I began by measuring the radio using digital callipers, and creating a faceplate in Fusion 360. The facecplate consisted of a hole for the dial, and a hole for the antenna. I then recreated the faceplate, adding ridges to put it inside. I also added buttons and a hole for the speaker. Once that was done, I created the backplate using a similar iteration method, and made it so the backplate fit using friction.

The third part of this project was to laser cut a wooden case. Using my plastic case as a baseline for the design, I added finger joints to combine the pieces. This had its own issues, primarily with coverage, as the original design utilized two pieces that allowed for better overhang. Despite this the new wooden design worked well. If I had more time to iterate on it, I would have attempted to figure out solutions to the coverage issue. I learned lightburn and fusion conversion well through this as well!
